What is STEM Racing

ENGINEERING
MEETS THE
TRACK

STEM Racing (formerly F1 in Schools) is the world's largest STEM competition for students aged 9–19, endorsed by Formula 1® and active in over 65 countries.

Teams of 3–6 students design, manufacture, and race a miniature CO₂-powered F1 car on a 20-metre track — applying real engineering principles used by professional motorsport teams.

But it's more than speed. Teams are judged on their engineering portfolio, project management, branding, verbal presentation, and pit display — a complete simulation of bringing a product from concept to competition.

Winners advance from regional → national → World Finals, held annually at a Formula One Grand Prix venue.

The Race

HOW IT
WORKS

The vehicle
A miniature F1 car. Real engineering challenges.
The car body is just 152mm long — slightly larger than a smartphone. It must be CNC-machined to exact tolerances, weigh at minimum 48g, incorporate front and rear wings, and use a CO₂ cartridge propulsion system. Every millimetre is a design decision.
80km/h top speed
0.916seconds — world record (20m)
Set by Australian team Infinitude in 2016. The number every team in every country races to beat. That benchmark drives every design decision Integrum makes.
Stage 01
Regional Finals
Teams compete locally against schools in their region. Judged on car speed, engineering portfolio, verbal presentation, and pit display quality.
Stage 02
National Finals
Top regional teams advance to compete nationally. Scrutiny increases sharply — engineering documentation, branding, and performance all matter equally.
The Goal
Stage 03
World Finals
Held at a Formula 1 Grand Prix venue. Teams from 65+ countries compete. The 2026 World Finals take place at Aramco's venue in Saudi Arabia. This is where Integrum is headed.
